The Concept

Visual Design + Concept Development + UX Design

The Trumpet is a editorial fashion e-commerce website inspired from Net-A-Porter. The goal of the project is to integrate editorial style magazine into an online format. The play in typography hierarchy and rendering typefaces for the web was exhibited in this piece. Exploration of a 12 column grid system in relation to web typography rules is communicated.

The Research

The Inspiration

Looking at editorial magazine layouts and how they used a layered and cut paper effect shaped the concept of The Trumpet. The website needed to have a luxurious feel but also needed personality and be relatable. The mixture of clean grid system and layered with images and playing with various types stimulated the site's goal to be "classy and experimental".

The Look & Feel

Logo Development

The inspiration of the logo was based on using typographic punctuation within the elements the logo as a statement. Fonts of Monstseratt Light, Avenir LT and Medium, and Mrs Eaves Roman were explored as the base fonts. The final decision to use Mrs. Eaves as this displayed a personal and humanistic feel while giving a subtle sense of fashion flare.

Typography Pairing

The decision for the Trumpet website is to pair Georgia with Orienta. The decision to pair these two together is due to the range of styles achieved within the upper and lowercase versions of both font family. Orienta displayed a clean, modern in the uppercase while itâ€™s lowercase displays humanist elements. Georgia family has classy and timeless feel which aligns nicely with the logo font choice of Mrs. Eaves.

Headliner Styles

Headliners within The Trumpet displayed mixes of uppercase, lowercase, italics, bolds. There was no set rules within the headlines except to play with the hierarchy and to mix the 2 font pairings using various font weights and styles within the family while experimenting with left and right alignments.
