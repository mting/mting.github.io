The Concept

UX Design + Web Design + Front End

A grass roots initiative that came from the passion and dedication of an artisan community. With possible corporate changes happening on Granville Island, the community banded together in solidarity to celebrate their cultural diversity through storytelling of the artisinal community. The challenge was to to find digital medium to capture the essence of their vision. The objective was to create a legacy newspaper, website and merchandise for the Granville Island business community that showcased the diversity of the businesses. This required the reconstruction a map of Granville Island, that would easily guide visitors around the Island and effectively locate the businesses on the Island.

The Channels

Newspaper & Magazine Spread

40,000 copies were printed initially and a second edition was commissioned. With this second edition, 60,000 were printed released in the Georgia Straight Magazine in February 2010 as an insert for the Olympics. Granville Island Works became the first marketing project funded by the the business community.

The Website

A WordPress website was built following the letterpress style of the magazine. The feel of the site is to showcase the multitude of talents on Granville Island. The website featured events, community blog, interactive map, newsletter sign up and organization for the ability to find businesses easily.

The Interactive Map

The map integrates flash animation with rollovers within the map section. It allows for users to search for businesses based on color coded locations with a brief description of their profile.

Social Media & Merchandise

Other channels was used to promote this project. A storytelling contest was launched in collaboration with Kiss Radio. Social media showcasing island events, built community. In addition merchandise such as t-shirts and bags were made.
