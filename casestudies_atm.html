The Concept

Visual Design + UX Design + User Research

ATM has several interfaces the public interacts to complete their daily commuting tasks which includes a touch screen kiosk and mobile app. In this project, the goal is to evaluate these user interfaces, identify the weaknesses and how to improve the overall user experience. How the public interacts with these interfaces, what types of main tasks the public completes, and understanding the user journey will define how these interfaces can improve to better the immediate usability.

The Research

The Methodology

Observation methods were used to gather information on how users interacted with ATM machines in Milan. A series of user personas were built based on daily commuting tasks in order to understand the mindset of the technical skills, goals and motivation of the user.

Learnability

  • Landing screen of touchscreen redundant
  • Too many options which causes confusion
  • Need simplification of text
  • Zoning structure does not make sense

Efficiency

  • Need breadcrumbs to know where you are
  • Too many steps to get to end goal
  • When do you get prompted to pay?

User Needs

  • Choose a destination
  • Choose type of ticket
  • Pay the ticket
  • Recharging the cards
  • Upgrade the card to a new area
  • View the map
  • Clear option on type of tickets to purchase
  • Discounts for student/senior

Interview Process

“It shouldn’t be complex. I want to get there and have all my options available to me.”

“I’d rather use the counter over the kiosk.”

“Too slow and time consuming.”

User Persona & Journey

A detailed user journey was mapped out based on the step by step flow and specific task of the user persona. This helped develop the features of the mobile app to solve the pain point of the commuter. Challenges in this process included how to restructure the amount of options and make it intuitive for the user persona to complete their task.

Low Fidelity Prototype

Low fidelity sketches were completed to display the main features of the mobile app. Several iteration were refined based on allowing users to complete their tasks in the most user centric manner.

The Look & Feel

Branding

The color palette used reflected the same style of ATM's current color palette. The look was refreashed with shades of grey. The tone of the app is friendly and approachable. Typeface of Barlow Condensed and Roboto was used for legibility and to give the app a more modern feel.
